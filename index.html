Module 01
The Main Causes of Food Poisoning
🦠
Bacteria & Toxins
Salmonella, Listeria, E. coli, Campylobacter, Staph aureus contaminate food and multiply rapidly.
🔬
Viruses
Norovirus and Hepatitis A transfer from infected food handlers; rapid-onset illness in tiny quantities.
⚗️
Chemical Contamination
Cleaning chemicals, pesticide residues, or non-food-grade materials introduced during handling.
🌿
Natural Toxins
Kidney beans, certain shellfish, spoiled fish (histamine) when undercooked or incorrectly stored.
🙌
Poor Personal Hygiene
Failure to wash hands, working while ill — leading contributors in aviation catering incidents.
🌡️
Incorrect Temperature
Wrong temperatures allow bacterial growth and toxin production; critical risk on long-haul flights.
Key symptoms: nausea, vomiting, diarrhoea, abdominal pain, fever — onset 30 minutes to 72 hours after ingestion.

Module 02 — Bacterial Growth
What bacteria need to multiply: F-A-T-T-O-M
F
Food
High-protein foods: meat, fish, eggs, dairy, rice, pasta
A
Acidity
Pathogens prefer neutral pH 6.5–7.5. Acidic foods inhibit growth
T
Temperature
5°C–63°C is the danger zone. Doubles every 20 mins at 37°C
T
Time
Safe → dangerous in as little as 2–4 hours
O
Oxygen
Most are aerobic. Anaerobes (e.g. C. botulinum) thrive without
M
Moisture
High water activity (Aw >0.85) supports growth. Dried = lower risk
Remove any one of these conditions and bacterial growth is severely limited. Temperature and time are the ones crew control directly.

Module 02 — Temperature
The Danger Zone
Between 5°C and 63°C, bacteria multiply at dangerous speed. At body temperature (37°C), some strains double every 20 minutes.
⚠ Never leave high-risk food in the danger zone for more than 2 hours cumulative.

Module 03
High-Risk Foods in Airline Catering
🍗
Cooked Meat & Poultry
Chicken, beef, pork — must remain cold-chain protected throughout service.
🐟
Cooked Seafood & Fish
Salmon, prawn dishes, sushi — highly perishable and temperature sensitive.
🧀
Dairy Products
Soft cheeses, yoghurt, cream sauces, custard — support Listeria and Staph growth.
🥚
Eggs & Egg Products
Quiches, mayo, egg-based salads — risk of Salmonella if temperature control fails.
🥗
Ready-to-Eat Salads
Pre-prepared with cooked components — no further heating, immediate contamination risk.
🍚
Cooked Rice & Pasta
Bacillus cereus spores survive cooking and produce toxins during cooling.
High-risk foods must NEVER be left at ambient temperature for more than 2 hours. Cold items below 5°C — hot items above 63°C.

Module 04
Control Measures — Food Hygiene & Safety
1
HACCP Principles
Identify hazards, set critical limits, monitor, verify, keep records. The backbone of airline catering safety.
2
Supplier Assurance
Only accept food from approved, audited suppliers. Check delivery seals, temperatures, and use-by dates.
3
Segregation
Keep raw and ready-to-eat items physically separated. Use dedicated colour-coded equipment.
4
Cleaning & Disinfection
Trolleys, surfaces, equipment cleaned per SOPs. Clean-as-you-go during all service.
5
Temperature Monitoring
Check and record chilled item temperatures on loading and during service. Report exceedances immediately.
6
Waste Management
Food waste bagged and secured. Never re-use or re-serve leftover food. Dispose per aircraft waste regulations.

Module 05
Cross-Contamination & Prevention
Direct Contact
Raw food (e.g. meat juice) directly contacts ready-to-eat food.
Via Hands
Touch raw food then handle RTE items without washing. Most common route in cabin crew scenarios.
Equipment Transfer
Same tongs/trays/surfaces for raw and RTE without cleaning between uses.
Drip Contamination
Liquids from high-risk items drip onto items stored below. Always store raw below RTE.
S
Separate
Keep raw and RTE foods apart at all times
S
Sanitise
Clean and disinfect surfaces and equipment
S
Safe Storage
Store correctly: raw below RTE, sealed containers
S
Safe Hands
Wash hands between handling different food types

Personal Hygiene Standards
  • ✓
    Report illness before duty — never handle food when unwell
  • ✓
    Nails short, clean, no polish or false nails during service
  • ✓
    No watches, rings, or jewellery (plain wedding band only)
  • ✓
    Hair tied back or covered at all times during service
  • ✓
    Clean uniform; cover cuts with brightly coloured waterproof dressing
  • ✓
    No eating, drinking, chewing gum near catering
  • ✓
    Avoid touching face, nose, hair while handling food
The 6-Step Hand Washing Procedure
Minimum 20 seconds with soap and warm water.
1Wet & Soap
2Palm to Palm
3Back of Hands
4Interlace Fingers
5Thumbs & Nails
6Rinse & Dry
Dry with a single-use paper towel. Wash hands: before food handling · after using the toilet · after touching raw food · after handling waste · after coughing or sneezing.

Module 08 — Completing the Module
Unfit Food Procedure & Key Messages
Food Complaint Procedure
1
STOP — Remove the item from service immediately. Do not discard it.
2
ISOLATE — Sealed bag: label with flight number, date, seat number, description.
3
INFORM — Notify the Senior Cabin Crew Member / Purser immediately.
4
DOCUMENT — Complete a Catering Non-Conformance / Incident Report.
5
PRESERVE — Retain for investigation. Hand to ground staff per SOPs.
6
REPORT — Submit to Food Safety / Quality team before end of duty.
6 Non-Negotiable Key Messages
🙌
Wash your hands. Before food handling, after the toilet, after touching raw food.
🌡️
Control temperature. Never leave high-risk food in the danger zone (5°C–63°C) for more than 2 hours.
⚠️
Prevent cross-contamination. Separate raw from RTE. Sanitise surfaces. Keep equipment clean.
🤒
Report illness. Do not handle food if unwell with vomiting, diarrhoea, or skin infections.
🚫
Reject & report unfit food. If in doubt, take it out — isolate, document, report.
📋
Follow procedures. SOPs exist to protect passengers and crew. Know them. Follow them. Every flight.

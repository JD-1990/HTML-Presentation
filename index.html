Causes of Food Poisoning

🦠 Bacteria & Toxins
The most common cause. Multiplies rapidly in warm, moist food environments.
🔬 Viruses
Norovirus and Hepatitis A can survive on surfaces and spread via touch.
⚗️ Chemicals
Cleaning products, pesticides or improper packaging contaminating food.
🍄 Natural Toxins
Found in certain mushrooms, shellfish and improperly stored starchy foods.
🙌 Poor Hygiene
Unwashed hands, sneezing over food or working while ill transfers pathogens.
🌡️ Wrong Temperature
Leaving food in the Danger Zone (5–63°C) allows bacteria to multiply fast.

Meet the Enemy — Key Bacteria

Salmonella
Found in: Raw poultry, eggs, dairy
Symptoms: Diarrhoea, vomiting, fever
Onset: 6–72 hours
Listeria
Found in: Soft cheese, deli meats, smoked fish
Symptoms: Flu-like, serious in pregnancy
Onset: 3–70 days
E.coli O157
Found in: Raw/undercooked beef, unpasteurised milk
Symptoms: Severe cramps, bloody diarrhoea
Onset: 1–8 days
Staphylococcus
Found in: Skin, nose, hands — transferred to food
Symptoms: Rapid-onset vomiting
Onset: 1–6 hours
Campylobacter
Found in: Raw poultry, untreated water
Symptoms: Diarrhoea, cramps, fever
Onset: 2–5 days
Remember
All five are invisible and tasteless. You cannot tell by looking or smelling whether food is safe.

FATTOM — Conditions for Bacterial Growth

F
Food
Bacteria need nutrients — proteins & carbohydrates
A
Acidity
Most pathogens thrive at neutral pH (6–7)
T
Temperature
Danger Zone: 5°C to 63°C
T
Time
2+ hours in Danger Zone = risk. Numbers double every 20 min
O
Oxygen
Some bacteria need oxygen; others thrive without it
M
Moisture
High water activity in food supports rapid growth

High Risk Foods

High risk foods are ready to eat — they will not be cooked or reheated before consumption. Any contamination will reach the passenger directly.
🥩 Cooked Meat & Poultry
Risk: Salmonella, Campylobacter, E.coli
🐟 Cooked Fish & Seafood
Risk: Listeria, Vibrio, Staphylococcus
🥛 Dairy Products
Risk: Listeria, Salmonella, E.coli
🥚 Eggs & Egg Dishes
Risk: Salmonella — especially in raw or runny preparations
🥗 Prepared Salads
Risk: E.coli, Listeria, Norovirus from cross contamination
🍱 Ready-Made Meals
Risk: Any pathogen if temperature chain is broken

Cross Contamination

How it spreads

  • ✋ Hands — touching raw food then ready-to-eat food without washing
  • 🔪 Equipment — using the same utensils for raw and cooked items
  • 📦 Packaging — leaking raw meat juices onto other products
  • 🪰 Environment — pests, unclean surfaces, airborne droplets

The 4 S's — Prevention

  • 🧼 Separate — keep raw and ready-to-eat foods completely apart
  • 🌡️ Store — raw below cooked in refrigerators, covered and labelled
  • 🧽 Sanitise — clean and disinfect all surfaces and equipment between uses
  • ✅ Sequence — handle ready-to-eat food before raw; never reverse

Personal Hygiene

Crew Hygiene Rules

  • 🙌 Wash hands before handling food and after every interruption
  • 🤒 Report illness — do not handle food if vomiting or with diarrhoea
  • 💅 No nail polish, false nails or jewellery when handling food
  • 👕 Wear clean uniform; use gloves correctly and change them often
  • 🩹 Cover all cuts and wounds with a blue waterproof dressing
  • 💇 Tie back long hair; do not cough or sneeze over food

6-Step Hand Washing

1
Wet hands with warm running water
2
Apply soap — enough to lather
3
Rub palms together vigorously
4
Clean between fingers & thumbs
5
Scrub backs of hands & wrists
6
Rinse thoroughly, dry on clean towel — 20 seconds minimum

Temperature Control

❄️ Frozen
-18°C or below
Bacteria dormant — not killed. Never refreeze thawed food.
🌿 Chilled
1°C – 5°C
Slows bacterial growth. Check fridge temps at every service.
⚠️ Danger Zone
5°C – 63°C
Bacteria multiply rapidly. 2-hour rule applies — no exceptions.
🔥 Reheating
75°C or above
Core temperature must reach 75°C. Reheat once only.
♨️ Hot Hold
63°C or above
Maintain throughout service. Check with probe thermometer.
⏱ The Golden Rule — Never leave food in the Danger Zone for more than 2 hours

Unfit Food & Reporting

Signs of Unfit Food

  • 👃 Unusual, sour or off smell
  • 🎨 Discolouration or mould visible
  • 📅 Past use-by or best-before date
  • 🌡️ Food found outside correct temperature range
  • 📦 Damaged, swollen or leaking packaging
  • 🐛 Evidence of pest contamination

6-Step Reporting Procedure

1
Stop — immediately cease use of the suspect food
2
Isolate — segregate from all other food; do not discard
3
Inform — notify the senior crew member immediately
4
Label — mark clearly as unfit; add date, flight number
5
Document — complete the food safety report form
6
Retain — keep the sample for investigation; never discard evidence

Module 01
The Main Causes of Food Poisoning
🦠
Bacteria & Toxins
The most common cause. Salmonella, Listeria, E. coli, Campylobacter, and Staph aureus contaminate food and multiply rapidly.
🔬
Viruses
Norovirus and Hepatitis A can transfer from infected food handlers. Rapid-onset illness even in very small quantities.
⚗️
Chemical Contamination
Cleaning chemicals, pesticide residues, or non-food-grade materials introduced into food during handling or storage.
🌿
Natural Toxins
Some foods contain toxins when undercooked or incorrectly stored — kidney beans, certain shellfish, spoiled fish (histamine).
🙌
Poor Personal Hygiene
Failure to wash hands, working while ill, and improper handling — leading contributors in aviation catering incidents.
🌡️
Incorrect Temperature
Storing or serving food at wrong temperatures allows bacterial growth and toxin production — critical risk on long-haul flights.
Key symptoms: nausea, vomiting, diarrhoea, abdominal pain, fever — onset 30 minutes to 72 hours after ingestion.

Module 02 — Bacterial Growth
What bacteria need to multiply: F-A-T-T-O-M
F
Food
High-protein foods: meat, fish, eggs, dairy, rice, pasta
A
Acidity
Pathogens prefer neutral pH 6.5–7.5. Acidic foods inhibit growth
T
Temperature
5°C–63°C is the danger zone. Double every 20 mins at 37°C
T
Time
Safe → dangerous level in as little as 2–4 hours
O
Oxygen
Most are aerobic. Anaerobes (e.g. C. botulinum) thrive without
M
Moisture
High water activity (Aw >0.85) supports growth. Dried = lower risk
Remove any one of these conditions and bacterial growth is severely limited. Temperature and time are the ones crew control directly.

Module 02 — Temperature
The Danger Zone
Between 5°C and 63°C, bacteria multiply at dangerous speed. At body temperature (37°C), some strains double every 20 minutes.
⚠ Never leave high-risk food in the danger zone for more than 2 hours cumulative.
≥ 63°C
Hot Holding / Reheating
Core temp must reach 75°C before serving. Hold at ≥63°C throughout service.
5–63°C
⚠ THE DANGER ZONE
Bacteria multiply rapidly. Max 2 hours cumulative. Discard if exceeded.
HIGH RISK
≤ 5°C
Chilled Storage
All high-risk chilled items. Check on loading. Do not serve if exceeded.
≤ −18°C
Frozen Storage
Must remain frozen at all times. Report any thawing immediately.

Module 03
High-Risk Foods in Airline Catering
🍗
Cooked Meat & Poultry
Chicken, beef, pork dishes — must remain cold-chain protected throughout service.
🐟
Cooked Seafood & Fish
Salmon, prawn dishes, sushi — highly perishable and temperature sensitive.
🧀
Dairy Products
Soft cheeses, yoghurt, cream sauces, custard — support Listeria and Staph growth.
🥚
Eggs & Egg Products
Quiches, mayo, egg-based salads — risk of Salmonella if temperature control fails.
🥗
Ready-to-Eat Salads
Pre-prepared with cooked components — no further heating. Immediate contamination risk.
🍚
Cooked Rice & Pasta
Bacillus cereus spores survive cooking and produce toxins during cooling.
High-risk foods must NEVER be left at ambient temperature for more than 2 hours. Cold items below 5°C — hot items above 63°C.

Module 04
Control Measures — Food Hygiene & Safety
1
HACCP Principles
Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points — identify hazards, set critical limits, monitor, verify, and keep records. The backbone of airline catering safety.
2
Supplier Assurance
Only accept food from approved, audited catering suppliers. Check delivery seals, temperatures, and use-by dates at point of loading.
3
Segregation
Keep raw and ready-to-eat items physically separated throughout. Use dedicated, colour-coded equipment where provided.
4
Cleaning & Disinfection
Trolleys, surfaces, and equipment cleaned and disinfected per SOPs. Clean-as-you-go principle applies during all service.
5
Temperature Monitoring
Check and record chilled item temperatures on loading and during service. Report any exceedances immediately per non-conformance procedure.
6
Waste Management
Food waste must be bagged and secured. Never re-use or re-serve leftover food from a completed flight. Dispose per aircraft waste regulations.

Module 05
Cross-Contamination & Prevention
Direct Contact
Raw food (e.g. meat juice) directly contacts ready-to-eat food. Example: raw protein leaking onto salad items in the trolley.
Via Hands
Touch raw food, then handle ready-to-eat items without washing. Most common route in cabin crew service scenarios.
Equipment Transfer
Using same tongs, trays, or surfaces for raw and RTE items without cleaning and disinfection between uses.
Drip Contamination
Liquids from high-risk items dripping onto items stored below. Always store raw items below ready-to-eat.
Separate
Keep raw and RTE foods apart at all times
Sanitise
Clean and disinfect surfaces and equipment
Safe Storage
Store correctly — raw below RTE, sealed containers
Safe Hands
Wash hands between handling different food types

Module 06
Personal Hygiene Standards
  • Report illness before duty — never handle food when unwell
  • Nails short, clean, no polish or false nails during service
  • No watches, rings, or jewellery (plain wedding band only)
  • Hair tied back or covered at all times during service
  • Clean uniform; cover cuts with brightly coloured waterproof dressing
  • No eating, drinking, chewing gum, or smoking near catering
  • Avoid touching face, nose, hair, or clothing while handling food

Temperature Control — Requirements & Actions
Zone Temperature Requirement Action if Exceeded
Frozen Storage ≤ −18°C Frozen items must remain frozen at all times Remove from service; report to senior crew
Chilled Storage ≤ 5°C All high-risk chilled items; check on loading Do not serve; quarantine; complete non-conformance report
⚠ Danger Zone 5°C – 63°C Minimise time in this range; max 2 hours cumulative Discard if 2-hour limit exceeded or in doubt
Hot Holding ≥ 63°C Hot food must reach 75°C core before serving; hold at ≥63°C Reheat fully or discard; never serve lukewarm hot food
Galley Ambient ≤ 25°C Keep service area cool; limit door-open time Monitor; minimise exposure of perishables
Golden Rule: When in doubt, throw it out. Never take risks with temperature-abused food — passenger safety is paramount.

Module 08 — Completing the Module
Unfit Food Procedure & Key Messages
Food Complaint Procedure
1
STOP
Remove the item from service immediately. Do not discard it.
2
ISOLATE
Sealed bag — label with flight number, date, seat number, description.
3
INFORM
Notify the Senior Cabin Crew Member / Purser immediately.
4
DOCUMENT
Complete a Cabin Safety Report (CSR) or Air Safety Report (ASR) as appropriate (paper or digital EFB).
5
PRESERVE
Retain for investigation. Hand to ground staff per SOPs for sample retention.
6
REPORT
Submit to Food Safety / Quality team before end of duty.
6 Non-Negotiable Key Messages
🙌
Wash your hands. Before food handling, after the toilet, after touching raw food.
🌡️
Control temperature. Never leave high-risk food in the danger zone (5°C–63°C) for more than 2 hours.
⚠️
Prevent cross-contamination. Separate raw from RTE. Sanitise surfaces. Keep equipment clean.
🤒
Report illness. Do not handle food if unwell with vomiting, diarrhoea, or skin infections.
🚫
Reject & report unfit food. If in doubt, take it out — isolate, document, report.
📋
Follow procedures. SOPs exist to protect passengers and crew. Know them. Follow them. Every flight.
